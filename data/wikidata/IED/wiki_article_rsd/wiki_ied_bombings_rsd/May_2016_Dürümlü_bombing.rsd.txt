The May 2016 Dürümlü bombing was a truck bombing that took place in Dürümlü hamlet in Diyarbakir's Sur district, Turkey on 12 May 2016 at approximately 22:30 local time. As a result of the attack 16 villagers were killed and 23 villagers were wounded. The People's Defence Forces (HPG), the military wing of the Kurdistan Workers' Party (PKK) accepted that the truck was driven by members of the HPG.But the HPG also explained that the explosives should have detonated elsewhere and that the truck only detonated as villagers opened fire on the truck.
